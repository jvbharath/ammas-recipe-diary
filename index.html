A digital home for Amma's handwritten recipes

From a mother's notebook to a family cookbook website

Before her daughter left home after marriage to begin a new life in the U.S., Amma wrote down the flavors of home — page by page — so comfort, memory, and everyday cooking could travel with her.

From Amma's notebook

“When home becomes far away, these recipes become memory, comfort, and love served warm.”

A notebook packed with love, spice, and reassurance

Some recipes came from Amma's South Indian kitchen, others were North Indian favorites and dishes the family loved over time. They were not restaurant recipes. They were the real meals of home — curries, dals, fries, kootu, gravies, and simple side dishes cooked in a kadai and remembered by feel.

Preserved for the next generation

This website keeps those handwritten pages alive in a new form: easier to browse, easier to cook from, and still tied to the warmth of the original diary.
